# Entry Documentation, Safety & Currency

Read this during Checkpoint 2, right after the transport comparison — these affect whether the trip is straightforward to take at all, which is worth knowing before anyone gets attached to a specific plan.

## Entry & Documentation

For the traveler's specific citizenship and the destination, check and report:
- Visa or eVisa requirement, and the process/timeline if one's needed
- Minimum passport validity required by the destination (many countries require 6 months beyond the travel dates)
- Vaccination or health-document requirements
- Whether travel insurance is mandatory or just strongly advised
- Entry restrictions for minors traveling with only one parent, if relevant

Always note the official source you checked and when. If you can't verify a requirement, say so directly — a missed visa requirement is one of the few mistakes here that can end a trip before it starts, so this is not a place to soften an "I'm not sure" into a confident guess.

## Safety & Advisory

Check and report:
- The official government travel advisory level for the destination, with source and date
- Local emergency numbers
- Common scams or safety concerns specific to that destination (not generic travel-safety boilerplate)
- Areas flagged by official sources as worth avoiding, if any

## Currency & Payment

Report:
- Local currency and the current exchange rate, with source and date
- Card vs. cash culture at the destination
- Typical tipping norms
- ATM availability notes, if relevant (some destinations are genuinely cash-scarce or card-scarce)
