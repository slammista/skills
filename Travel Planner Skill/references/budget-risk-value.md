# Budget Calibration, Risk & Value

Read this during Checkpoint 3 when building the budget scenarios.

## Worldwide Budget Calibration

A fixed dollar figure means completely different things in different places — "$50/day, balanced" is a comfortable mid-range budget in much of Southeast Asia and barely covers a single meal in central Tokyo or Zurich. Before generating any budget numbers:

1. Work out the destination's relative cost level — Low / Medium / High / Very High — using cost-of-living sources (Numbeo, Budget Your Trip, Nomad List).
2. Calibrate the Budget / Balanced / Premium scenarios to *that* level, not a generic global number.
3. Show totals in both the traveler's home currency and the local currency, with the exchange rate and date used.
4. If the trip spans multiple destinations with different cost levels, calibrate each leg on its own rather than averaging across them.

## Smart Budget Engine

Build three scenarios — Budget, Balanced, Premium — each covering transport, accommodation, food, activities, local mobility, and hidden costs, with totals in both currencies.

## Hidden Cost Detector

These are the costs people forget to budget for, and they add up fast. Check for: tourist taxes, baggage fees, parking, fuel, tolls, booking fees, resort fees, airport/port transfers, ferry supplements, and mandatory entry fees (city tourist taxes, national park fees, etc.).

## Travel Risk Engine

Rate each of the following Low / Medium / High, with sourcing where you can find it: weather risk, strikes, congestion, seasonal demand spikes, ferry/flight disruption patterns, local events overlapping the trip, and the official safety advisory level already gathered in Checkpoint 2.

## Value Optimizer

Look for genuinely better options you found while researching — cheaper travel dates, cheaper routes, better-value accommodation areas, fewer-transfer routings, cheaper local mobility — and calculate the savings from the actual numbers compared, not a guessed percentage. If nothing better turned up, say that too; manufacturing a savings figure to seem thorough defeats the point.
