# Sourcing Guide

Read this when you're about to state a price, schedule, visa rule, weather forecast, or any other fact that changes over time. The short version: check it if you can, and say so if you can't.

## Why this matters more than it might seem

Nobody asks a travel consultant for numbers out of curiosity — they're about to spend money based on what you say. A flight price that's $150 off, a visa rule that's actually out of date, or a "no rain expected" that ignores monsoon season doesn't just look bad, it can cost someone real money or a missed connection. That's the bar: would you be comfortable if the traveler booked something today based on this exact number?

## When to actually check

Check before stating, for the specific destination and dates in question (not from memory alone, since these all change):
- Flight, rail, bus, and ferry prices and schedules
- Accommodation price ranges by area
- Visa/entry requirements, passport validity rules, vaccination requirements
- Government travel advisories / safety levels
- Current weather and seasonal patterns for the travel dates
- Currency exchange rates
- Strikes, closures, local events, or festivals near the travel dates
- Typical daily cost-of-living for that specific destination

## Where to look, by data type

These are categories, not a fixed list — use whatever in each category is actually accessible to you, and don't force a single brand if a better regional fit exists (e.g. a Southeast Asia bus route is better served by a regional aggregator than a European one).

| Data type | Source category |
|---|---|
| Flights | Flight metasearch engines (Google Flights, Skyscanner, Kayak, etc.) and airline sites |
| Rail / bus | Regional booking aggregators (Omio, Trainline, Rome2Rio, 12Go in Asia, etc.) and national operator sites |
| Ferries | Ferry operator sites and regional ferry aggregators (Direct Ferries, etc.) |
| Accommodation | Booking.com, Google Hotels, Hostelworld, Airbnb, Expedia |
| Car rental / local mobility | Rental aggregators (DiscoverCars, RentalCars, etc.), rideshare apps' published fare estimators |
| Cost-of-living / daily budget | Numbeo, Budget Your Trip, Nomad List |
| Visas / entry requirements | Official government visa portals (destination *and* the traveler's home country), IATA Travel Centre |
| Safety / advisories | Official government travel advisory sites (gov.uk travel advice, travel.state.gov, smartraveller.gov.au, or the traveler's own country's equivalent) |
| Weather | National meteorological services, or major aggregators (AccuWeather, Weather.com) |
| Currency | XE, Google currency converter, or equivalent |
| Local events / strikes / disruptions | Recent local news search |

## How to show your sourcing

Don't just state a figure — tag it lightly so the traveler can tell what's verified:

> Round-trip flight ≈ $420 (flight metasearch, checked today)
> Tourist visa not required for stays under 90 days (official visa portal, checked today)

If a search comes back empty or you genuinely can't verify something, say so instead of softening it into a vague-but-confident number:

> Estimate — couldn't verify this one. Confirm before booking.

## Freshness

If the destination, dates, or scope change mid-conversation, re-check rather than reusing an earlier number — a price you found for May isn't the price for August.
